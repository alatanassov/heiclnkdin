Accreditation Bodies & IAF CertSearch

Validating global AI certifications relies on a strict hierarchy of trust. Self-declarations are insufficient for high-risk AI. This section outlines how bodies like UKAS (United Kingdom Accreditation Service) and ANAB (ANSI National Accreditation Board) empower the ecosystem, utilizing IAF CertSearch as the single source of truth.

🏢

Tier 1: Accreditation Bodies

Organizations like UKAS and ANAB evaluate Certification Bodies (CBs). They ensure the auditors themselves are competent in AI risks, bias testing, and data governance before they are allowed to issue ISO 42001 certificates.

Tier 2: Certification Bodies

BSI, SGS, TÜV, etc. These bodies conduct actual audits of tech companies. Only certificates issued by an accredited CB carry international weight and regulatory recognition.

IAF CertSearch

The global database. If an AI company claims ISO 42001 certification, procuring entities and regulators query IAF CertSearch. If it's not there, it's not valid, preventing "AI washing" and fraudulent claims.

Synergy with NIST AI Risk Management Framework

While NIST provides a flexible taxonomy for AI risks (Govern, Map, Measure, Manage), ISO 42001 provides the auditable, structural requirements to ensure those NIST functions are actually executed consistently.

NIST Core Function ISO/IEC 42001 Clauses & Controls Integration Strategy
GOVERN Clause 5 (Leadership), Annex A.2 (AI policies) Use ISO leadership clauses to mandate the creation of the cross-functional AI governance boards recommended by NIST.
MAP Clause 4 (Context), Clause 6.1 (Risk Assessment) Deploy NIST's context-gathering exercises to fulfill ISO's requirement to understand stakeholder needs and system context.
MEASURE Clause 9 (Performance Eval), Annex A.7 (Data/Metrics) Implement NIST's specific quantitative metrics for bias/robustness as the KPIs required by ISO Clause 9.
MANAGE Clause 8 (Operation), Annex A.4 (Risk Treatment) Document the risk mitigations derived from NIST into the formal ISO Statement of Applicability (SoA).

Compliance Engine for the EU AI Act

ISO 42001 is positioned as the primary mechanism for demonstrating compliance with the EU AI Act, particularly for high-risk systems requiring formal conformity assessments.

  • ✓

    Article 17 Quality Management System (QMS)

    The EU Act explicitly requires providers of High-Risk AI to establish a QMS. ISO 42001 is explicitly designed to fulfill this requirement, covering documentation, post-market monitoring, and accountability.

  • ✓

    Conformity Assessments

    Under the EU Act, having a certified QMS (like ISO 42001) streamlines the conformity assessment process required before placing a high-risk system on the EU market, effectively acting as a fast-track presumption of conformity for management processes.

  • ✓

    General Purpose AI (GPAI)

    While GPAI has separate tiered requirements in the Act, ISO 42001's focus on transparency, systemic risk mapping, and continuous evaluation directly addresses the governance needs placed on foundational model providers.
